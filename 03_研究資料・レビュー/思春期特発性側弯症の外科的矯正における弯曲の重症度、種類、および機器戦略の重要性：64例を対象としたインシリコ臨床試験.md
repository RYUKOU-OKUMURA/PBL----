description: "Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis is a three-dimensional deformity of the spine which is frequently corrected with the implantation of instrumentation with generally good or excellent clinical results; mechanical post-operative complications such as implant loosening and breakage are however relatively frequent. The rate of complications is associated with a lack of consensus about the surgical decision-making process; choices about the instrumentation length, the anchoring implants and the degree of correction are indeed mostly based on personal views and previous experience of the surgeon. In this work, we performed an in silico clinical trial on a large number of subjects in order to clarify which factors have the highest importance in determining the risk of complications by quantitatively analysing the mechanical stresses and loads in the instrumentation after the correction maneuvers. The results of the simulations highlighted the fundamental role of the curve severity, also in its three-dimensional aspect, and of the instrumentation strategy, whereas the length of the fixation had a lower importance."

## Introduction導入

Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS) is a three-dimensional deformity of the spine appearing during puberty and showing largely variable severity and curve patterns among the affected individuals<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 1" title="Lenke, L. G. et al. Curve prevalence of a new classification of operative adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: Does classification correlate with treatment?. Spine 27, 604–611 (2002)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR1" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e374">1</a></sup>. In select cases, AIS can be treated conservatively by means of physiotherapy and/or bracing, with the aim of stopping the progression of the curves<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 2" title="Weiss, H.-R. et al. Physical exercises in the treatment of idiopathic scoliosis at risk of brace treatment–SOSORT consensus paper 2005. Scoliosis 1, 6 (2006)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR2" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e378">2</a></sup>. In general, less severe curves tend to have a lower risk of progression<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 3" title="Skalli, W. et al. Early detection of progressive adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: A severity index. Spine 42, 823–830 (2017)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR3" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e382">3</a></sup>; for this reason, surgical correction is considered in skeletally immature subjects in which the Cobb angle in the coronal plane exceeds a certain threshold, typically 50°<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 4" title="Weiss, H.-R. Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS)—an indication for surgery? A systematic review of the literature. Disabil. Rehabil. 30, 799–807 (2008)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR4" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e386">4</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 5" title="Hopf, C. Criteria for treatment of idiopathic scoliosis between 40 degrees and 50 degrees. Surgical vs. conservative therapy. Orthopade 29, 500–506 (2000)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR5" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e389">5</a></sup>. Nevertheless, surgical treatment is nowadays also considered in less severe cases, down to Cobb angles of 35°<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 4" title="Weiss, H.-R. Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS)—an indication for surgery? A systematic review of the literature. Disabil. Rehabil. 30, 799–807 (2008)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR4" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e393">4</a></sup>, and leads in most cases to very good or excellent clinical and cosmetic results<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 6" title="Weinstein, S. L., Dolan, L. A., Cheng, J. C. Y., Danielsson, A. &amp; Morcuende, J. A. Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. Lancet 371, 1527–1537 (2008)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR6" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e398">6</a></sup>. Indeed, the technologies and implants currently available to the surgeons allow for a safe reduction of the coronal and three-dimensional deformities while preserving a correct sagittal alignment<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 6" title="Weinstein, S. L., Dolan, L. A., Cheng, J. C. Y., Danielsson, A. &amp; Morcuende, J. A. Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. Lancet 371, 1527–1537 (2008)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR6" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e402">6</a></sup>.  
思春期特発性側弯症（AIS）は、思春期に現れる脊椎の三次元変形であり、罹患した個人の間で重症度や湾曲パターンが大きく異なります1。一部のケースでは、AIS は弯曲の進行を止めることを目的として、理学療法や装具療法によって保存的に治療できます 2。一般に、弯曲の重度が低いほど進行のリスクが低い傾向があります3。このため、前頭面のコブ角が特定の閾値 (通常は 50°) を超える骨格的に未熟な被験者では、外科的矯正が考慮されます4,5。それにもかかわらず、今日では、コブ角 35°4 までのそれほど重度でない症例でも外科的治療が考慮されており、ほとんどの場合、非常に良好または優れた臨床的および美容上の結果が得られます 6。実際、外科医が現在利用できる技術とインプラントは、正しい矢状方向のアライメントを維持しながら、冠状変形と三次元変形を安全に整復することを可能にします6。

The use of instrumentation, however, is associated with peri-operative and post-operative complications related with the challenging biomechanical environment, especially after the correction of severe deformities. Bartley et al. described cases of intra-operative loss of fixation to bone, disassembly of implants, and broken rods while performing aggressive correction manoeuvres<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 7" title="Bartley, C. E. et al. Perioperative and delayed major complications following surgical treatment of adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. J. Bone Jt. Surg. Am. 99, 1206–1212 (2017)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR7" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e409">7</a></sup>. In addition, Weiss and Goodall described a varied but high rate of post-operative complications such as mechanical failure of the instrumentation, pseudoarthrosis, screw loosening, loss of correction, and several others<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Weiss, H.-R. &amp; Goodall, D. Scoliosis 3, 9 (2008)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR8" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e413">8</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Wimmer, C. &amp; Gluch, H. Aseptic loosening after CD instrumentation in the treatment of scoliosis: A report about eight cases. J. Spinal Disord. 11, 440–443 (1998)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR9" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e413_1">9</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Abul-Kasim, K. &amp; Ohlin, A. Evaluation of implant loosening following segmental pedicle screw fixation in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: A 2 year follow-up with low-dose CT. Scoliosis 9, 13 (2014)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR10" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e413_2">10</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 11" title="Uehara, M. et al. Pedicle screw loosening after posterior spinal fusion for adolescent idiopathic scoliosis in upper and lower instrumented vertebrae having major perforation. Spine 42, 1895–1900 (2017)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR11" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e416">11</a></sup>. Delayed complications associated with the posterior rods are relatively common; Flynn et al. observed radiographic adverse events, including rod breakage, loss of correction as well as proximal screw pull-out in 6.8% of patients treated with posterior instrumentation for Lenke type 1 AIS<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 12" title="Flynn, J. M., Betz, R. R., O’Brien, M. F., Newton, P. O. &amp; Harms Study Group. Radiographic classification of complications of instrumentation in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. Clin. Orthop. Relat. Res. 468, 665–669 (2010)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR12" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e420">12</a></sup>.  
しかし、器具の使用は、特に重度の変形の矯正後、困難な生体力学的環境に関連した周術期および術後の合併症を伴います。バートリーら。彼らは、積極的な矯正操作を行っている間に、術中の骨への固定の喪失、インプラントの分解、およびロッドの破損のケースを説明しました7。さらに、Weiss と Goodall は、器具の機械的故障、偽関節症、ネジの緩み、矯正の喪失、その他いくつかの合併症など、さまざまではあるが高率の術後合併症について述べています8、9、10、11。後桿体に関連する遅発性合併症は比較的一般的です。フリンら。レンケ 1 型 AIS12 の後部器具で治療を受けた患者の 6.8% で、ロッドの破損、矯正の喪失、近位ネジの抜け落ちなどの X 線撮影上の有害事象が観察されました。

As a matter of fact, although a considerable amount of studies about the surgical treatment of AIS has been published, the surgical decision-making, which is aimed at maximizing the clinical outcome while minimizing the risk of complications, still involves several open questions, even when restricting the options to posterior fixation only. The planning of the instrumentation length, the choice of the anchoring devices and their implantation patterns, i.e. skipping some levels or using more implants on the convex side, as well as the selection of the rod material and diameter remain indeed matters of debate. This lack of consensus has been highlighted in papers in which experienced spine deformity surgeons were asked to select the instrumentation strategy for some cases of AIS<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Robitaille, M., Aubin, C. E. &amp; Labelle, H. Intra and interobserver variability of preoperative planning for surgical instrumentation in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. Eur. Spine J. 16, 1604–1614 (2007)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR13" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e427">13</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Aubin, C.-E., Labelle, H. &amp; Ciolofan, O. C. Variability of spinal instrumentation configurations in adolescent idiopathic scoliosis. Eur. Spine J. 16, 57–64 (2007)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR14" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e427_1">14</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 15" title="Le Navéaux, F., Larson, A. The authors attributed this variability to personal views and preferences for the targets for correction, to previous experience, to the inter-rater variability in the classification systems used as a support for the planning<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 1" title="Lenke, L. G. et al. Curve prevalence of a new classification of operative adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: Does classification correlate with treatment?. Spine 27, 604–611 (2002)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR1" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e434">1</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 16" title="King, H. Am. 65, 1302–1313 (1983)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR16" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e437">16</a></sup> as well as to the lack of standardized strategies and rational rules based on biomechanical studies, which are currently relatively limited<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 14" title="Aubin, C.-E., Labelle, H. &amp; Ciolofan, O. Spine J. 16, 57–64 (2007)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR14" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e441">14</a></sup>.  
実際のところ、AIS の外科的治療に関するかなりの量の研究が発表されていますが、合併症のリスクを最小限に抑えながら臨床結果を最大化することを目的とした外科的意思決定には、依然としていくつかの未解決の疑問が残っています。オプションを後方固定のみに制限する場合。器具の長さの計画、固定装置とその埋め込みパターンの選択、つまり、いくつかのレベルをスキップするか、凸面側により多くのインプラントを使用するか、また、ロッドの材質と直径の選択は、依然として議論の余地があります。この合意の欠如は、経験豊富な脊椎変形外科医にAISの一部の症例に対する器具使用戦略の選択を依頼した論文で強調されている13、14、15。そこでは、さまざまな専門家によって提案された術前計画に大きなばらつきが見つかった、臨床転帰やインプラントの緩みや破損などの機械的合併症の発生という点で明らかな影響を及ぼします。著者らは、このばらつきの原因を、修正対象に対する個人的な見解や好み、過去の経験、計画のサポートとして使用される分類システムの評価者間のばらつき1,16、および標準化された戦略と合理的手法の欠如に起因すると考えた。生体力学の研究に基づいた規則ですが、現在は比較的限定されています14。

The aim of this paper is to provide a large set of biomechanical data to support the definition of standardized strategies and rules for the pre-operative planning of AIS surgery. By simulating the correction of a large number of AIS cases covering a wide range of severity scenarios, curve types as well as possible instrumentation strategies, we aimed at predicting the most important parameters determining high stresses and mechanical loads in the instrumentation, which were assumed to be associated with post-operative mechanical complications.  
この論文の目的は、AIS 手術の術前計画のための標準化された戦略とルールの定義をサポートする大規模な生体力学データを提供することです。広範囲の重症度シナリオ、曲線タイプ、および考えられる計装戦略をカバーする多数の AIS ケースの修正をシミュレートすることにより、私たちは、計装内の高応力と機械的負荷を決定する最も重要なパラメーターを予測することを目的としました。術後の機械的合併症を伴う可能性があります。

## Materials and methods材料と方法

### Patients and imaging患者と画像処理

This computational study was carried out in accordance with relevant guidelines and regulations. Fully anonymized biplanar radiographs acquired with the EOS Imaging System (EOS Imaging, Paris, France) were available for 192 subjects with age ranging from 10 to 18 years old from a previous clinical trial<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 17" title="Bassani, T., Stucovitz, E., Galbusera, F. &amp; Brayda-Bruno, M. Is rasterstereography a valid noninvasive method for the screening of juvenile and adolescent idiopathic scoliosis?. Eur. Spine J. 28, 526–535 (2019)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR17" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e460">17</a></sup> which was approved by the Ethics Committee of Ospedale San Raffaele, Milan (protocol EOSCC2013) and during which subject assent and parental permission to use anonymized data in successive studies were given by signing an informed consent. Among those, the 64 subjects having Cobb angle in the coronal plane larger than 35° were included in the study. The resulting cohort had a Cobb angles in the range between 36.8° and 84.6°, with a median value of 54.6°. The T4–T12 kyphosis ranged between 0.2° and 72.5° with a median of 25.9°, whereas the range of the L1–L5 lordosis was 3.2°–77.3° with a median value of 44.1°. The location of 55 relevant landmarks, 32 for each vertebra in the T1–L5 region and 11 for the pelvis, were automatically extracted from the three-dimensional reconstruction of the spinal anatomy obtained by means of sterEOS proprietary software (EOS Imaging, Paris, France) through a reverse-engineering approach. The extent of all scoliotic curves, as well as the curve type based on the Lenke classification<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 1" title="Lenke, L. G. et al. Curve prevalence of a new classification of operative adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: Does classification correlate with treatment?. Spine 27, 604–611 (2002)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR1" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e464">1</a></sup>, were then automatically determined by means of purposely developed Python scripts based on the location of the landmarks.  
この計算による研究は、関連するガイドラインと規制に従って実行されました。 EOS イメージング システム (EOS Imaging、フランス、パリ) で取得した完全に匿名化された二平面 X 線写真は、ミラノのサン ラッファエレ病院の倫理委員会によって承認された以前の臨床試験 17 から、10 歳から 18 歳までの年齢の 192 人の被験者に対して利用可能でした。 （プロトコルEOSCC2013）およびその期間中、継続的な研究で匿名化されたデータを使用するための被験者の同意と親の許可は、通知された同意書に署名することによって与えられました。 同意。このうち、前頭面のコブ角が 35°より大きい 64 名の被験者が研究に含まれました。得られたコホートのコブ角は 36.8° ～ 84.6° の範囲で、中央値は 54.6°でした。 T4～T12の後弯の範囲は0.2°～72.5°で中央値は25.9°でしたが、L1～L5の前弯の範囲は3.2°～77.3°で中央値は44.1°でした。 55 個の関連ランドマークの位置 (T1 ～ L5 領域の各椎骨に 32 個、骨盤に 11 個) は、sterEOS 独自のソフトウェア (EOS Imaging、パリ、フランス) によって得られた脊椎解剖学的構造の 3 次元再構成から自動的に抽出されました。 ）リバースエンジニアリングアプローチを通じて。その後、すべての側弯症曲線の程度と、レンケ分類 1 に基づく曲線のタイプが、ランドマークの位置に基づいて、意図的に開発された Python スクリプトによって自動的に決定されました。

### Finite element modeling有限要素モデリング

Simple finite element models of the T1-sacrum region of all spines aimed at simulating deformity correction with posterior instrumentation were automatically built (Fig. [1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#Fig1)). The models consisted exclusively of beam elements and simulated each vertebra and the pelvis as rigid structures, i.e. not capable of deforming under mechanical load, and intervertebral discs as beam elements with cross-sectional areas fitting the size of the adjacent endplates and fixed material properties (elastic modulus of 6 MPa, Poisson ratio of 0.45) mimicking the global behavior of the intervertebral disc<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 18" title="Yang, H., Jekir, M. G., Davis, M. W. &amp; Keaveny, T. M. Effective modulus of the human intervertebral disc and its effect on vertebral bone stress. J. Biomech. 49, 1134–1140 (2016)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR18" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e479">18</a></sup>. Facet joints and ligaments were not included in the models. The posterior rods were modeled as beam elements with a circular section, a diameter of 5.5 mm and the material properties of titanium (elastic modulus of 110 GPa, Poisson ratio of 0.3).  
後方器具による変形矯正をシミュレートすることを目的とした、すべての脊椎の T1 仙骨領域の単純な有限要素モデルが自動的に構築されました (図 1)。モデルはビーム要素のみで構成され、各椎骨と骨盤を剛構造、つまり機械的負荷の下では変形できないものとしてシミュレートし、椎間板を隣接する終板のサイズに適合する断面積と固定された材料特性を備えたビーム要素としてシミュレートしました (弾性率 6 MPa、ポアソン比 0.45）、椎間板の全体的な挙動を模倣します18。椎間関節と靱帯はモデルに含まれていません。後部ロッドは、円形断面、直径 5.5 mm、チタンの材料特性 (弾性率 110 GPa、ポアソン比 0.3) を持つビーム要素としてモデル化されました。

### Target configurations and instrumentationターゲットの構成と計測

A large number of correction and instrumentation scenarios were simulated for each spine model. In total, 16 target configurations, i.e. spine alignments to be obtained after deformity correction, were defined. The unaltered sagittal curvature with respect to the pre-correction condition was considered as baseline configuration, i.e. as reference for the generation of the models in which sagittal corrections were implemented. With respect to the baseline, increases of lumbar lordosis and thoracic kyphosis up to 30° in steps of 10°, in all the possible 15 permutations (0 degree of lordosis and 10 degree of kyphosis, 0° and 20°, etc.) were simulated. The target Cobb angle of all scoliotic curves in the coronal plane as well as the vertebral rotations in the transverse plane were set to zero in all 16 configurations.

Surgical correction of the deformity by means of polyaxial screws and posterior rods was modeled using an established approach<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 20" title="Galbusera, F. et al. Planning the surgical correction of spinal deformities: Toward the identification of the biomechanical principles by means of numerical simulation. Front. Bioeng. Biotechnol. 3, 178 (2015)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR20" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e548">20</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 21" title="Giudici, F. et al. Spine J. 26, 524–532 (2017)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR21" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e551">21</a></sup>, appropriately adapted to the beam models used in the current study. In brief, in the first loading step the nodes corresponding to the center of the pedicles were simultaneously displaced so that their final position matched the position of the pedicles in the target configuration; in the second step, these boundary conditions were released and kinematic couplings between the nodes of the pedicles and the closest nodes on the posterior rods were defined. As a result, the elastic energy due to the deformation imposed in the first step, not constrained anymore by the nodal boundary conditions at the pedicles, determined the strains and stresses in the instrumentation which were the subject of the present study. Rotational degrees of freedom were not constrained at the screw–rod junction.

### Data analysis and statisticsデータ分析と統計

## Discussion議論

In this paper, we estimated the relevance of several features describing the pre-operative shape of the spine, the targets for the surgical correction of the deformity as well as the surgical instrumentation in determining the mechanical stresses and loads in the instrumentation due to the correction maneuvers. In general, the most important predictors of high stresses were the rotation in the transverse place of the apical vertebra, the maximal Cobb angle in the coronal plane, and the screw pattern. Other predictors, such as Lenke type and UIV, had also a non-negligible importance. Such findings were obtained by means of a large simulation campaign on a high number of subjects and considering several instrumentation strategies and target configurations, with a general approach more influenced by big data analytics rather than by the consolidated techniques used in biomechanics. As a matter of fact, the present study constitutes an in silico clinical trial, a novel paradigm for the assessment of medical devices and surgical techniques which is already having a heavy impact on scientific studies and the medical industry<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 24" title="Viceconti, M. Biomechanics-based in silico medicine: The manifesto of a new science. J. Biomech. 48, 193–194 (2015)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR24" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e944">24</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 25" title="Viceconti, M., Henney, A. &amp; Morley-Fletcher, E. In silico clinical trials: How computer simulation will transform the biomedical industry. Int. J. Clin. While models replicating the anatomy and biomechanics of specific patients, i.e. patient-specific models, have been documented in numerous papers, in silico clinical trials are a relatively novel development that still needs to gain a wide adoption by the scientific community. Despite the similarities, the scope of the two paradigms is different: while patient-specific models aim at investigating one specific patient, condition, pathology or treatment to gain better knowledge or to guide the clinical decisions about that specific case, in silico trials aim at a higher level of evidence by providing statistical significance data about some quantities (efficacy, safety, etc.) in the investigated population<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 24" title="Viceconti, M. The simple beam-based modeling approach is a major limitation of this study, and should be accounted for in light of the need for the full automatization of model generation, execution and postprocessing. Indeed, automated scripts proved to be a necessity for the management of such a large population and of a high number of target configurations and instrumentation strategies; as a matter of fact, automatization is a key issue in large-scale in silico trials<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 25" title="Viceconti, M., Henney, A. &amp; Morley-Fletcher, E. Vertebrae were considered as rigid; intervertebral discs were modeled with a single beam element with material properties not accounting for the alterations due to the deformity<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 59" title="Little, J. Although similar approaches have been employed in previous studies<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Kiefer, A., Shirazi-Adl, A. &amp; Parnianpour, M. Biomech. 70, 33–42 (2018)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/?felosearch_translate=1#ref-CR65" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e1113_2">65</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Cui, J. Furthermore, all material properties were assigned on a patient-independent basis, whereas it is well known that different patients exhibit different degrees of spinal flexibility<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 68" title="Busscher, I. et al. Biomechanical characteristics of different regions of the human spine: An in vitro study on multilevel spinal segments. Spine 34, E76-82 (2009)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR59" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e1127">59</a></sup>, and that the mechanical properties of spinal tissues have a major role in determining the resulting spine biomechanics<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" title="Little, J. P. &amp; Adam, C. J. The effect of soft tissue properties on spinal flexibility in scoliosis: Biomechanical simulation of fulcrum bending. In this study, we nevertheless decided to employ this simplified approach in order to better focus on the importance of the spinal curvature and of the instrumentation strategy, leaving out the patient-dependent stiffness which could have acted as a confounding factor. Besides, the assessment of patient-specific properties in spinal deformity patients remains challenging and prone to uncertainties, and requires in any case additional radiographic images<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 57" title="Lafon, Y., Steib, J.-P. &amp; Skalli, W. Furthermore, the boundary conditions used to model the rod engagement allowed free rotations, which correctly mimicked the effect of polyaxial screws during correction but not the lock achieved after tightening the set screws. Finally, the algorithmic choice of the upper and lower instrumented vertebrae may have led to the simulation of a number of non-realistic or clinically non-acceptable instrumentation strategies, resulting in unsatisfactory corrections and possibly affecting the statistical analysis.

Although a thorough comparison of the results with previous numerical and in vivo studies was performed, an extensive validation of the models could not be achieved due to the lack of experimental biomechanical data directly investigating the effect of curvature and instrumentation strategies in AIS correction. This aspect is especially critical if in silico clinical trials are intended to be used as medical devices, i.e. to draw decisions impacting the clinical decisions for specific patients or to prove the validity of implants or surgical techniques for regulatory purposes. As a matter of fact, the models described in this paper as well as other AIS correction models reported in the literature would likely not fulfil the critera described in the ASME standard V V 40 about the credibility of computational models<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 72" title="The American Society of Mechanical Engineers. However, it should be noted that the present modelling framework was not intended to be used as a medical device but was rather aimed at providing a better understanding of the relevance of some parameters on the biomechanical outcome of the surgery, with no direct influence on specific patients or implants.

It should be noted that some of the limitations discussed above are shared with several available models of scoliosis correction, which also include rigid or beam elements and have a variable degree of sophistication<sup><a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 43" title="Aubin, C.-E. et al. Spine 37, E1479–E1487 (2012)." href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-81319-z#ref-CR44" id="ref-link-section-d46011444e1156">44</a>,<a data-track="click" data-track-action="reference anchor" data-track-label="link" data-test="citation-ref" aria-label="Reference 46" title="Delikaris, A. et al. With respect to the rigid-body models developed by the Montréal group, we did not account for the specific correction maneuvers, but we simply simultaneously imposed displacement boundary conditions to replicate, in a simplified way, the final corrected configuration. Despite all these limitations, the present dataset constitutes a highly valuable resource, especially considering that its size largely exceeds, in terms of both number of patients and surgical strategies, any biomechanical study about AIS correction previously published. Besides, it should be noted that the flexible simulation framework here presented would allow for a straightforward integration of functional loads applied after the deformity correction, such as compressive loads replicating standing, trunk flexion, carrying loads etc.

In conclusion, the fully automated simulation framework here described, which was employed on a large population of subjects with different curve types and severities, demonstrated that apical rotation, Cobb angle in the coronal plane and screw pattern are the most important predictors of high stresses and loads in the instrumentation after AIS correction. We believe that such large campaigns of biomechanical simulations, and in silico clinical trials in general, have an enormous potential for the study of AIS and for other pathologies exhibiting a high degree of inter-subject variability.
